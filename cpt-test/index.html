CPT Testing in Barnsley: Stratigraphic Profiling for South Yorkshire Ground Conditions

Together, we solve the challenges of tomorrow.

LEARN MORE →

The Coal Measures strata and overlying glacial tills beneath Barnsley demand a methodical approach to site investigation. Boreholes can tell part of the story, but when clients need a continuous, high-resolution profile of the ground, the CPT (Cone Penetration Test) becomes the instrument of choice. Our team has deployed CPT rigs across sites from the Dearne Valley to the M1 corridor, pushing through stiff boulder clays and into the weathered mudstone beneath. The data stream—tip resistance, sleeve friction, and pore pressure—gives engineers a near-instant read on stratigraphy without the disturbance of sampling. For schemes where soft alluvium overlies the Pennine Middle Coal Measures, combining the CPT with in-situ permeability testing clarifies drainage behaviour, while slope stability analysis is often needed where the valley sides show signs of historic landslip.

A single CPT sounding in Barnsley's glacial till delivers more stratigraphic resolution than a cluster of six SPT boreholes, at a fraction of the mobilisation time.

Methodology and scope

Barnsley sits at roughly 120 metres above sea level, with a landscape shaped by the River Dearne and centuries of mining. Much of the town centre and surrounding industrial parks rest on a complex sequence of made ground, glacial deposits, and Carboniferous bedrock. A CPT rig can push through these transitions in a single day, logging cone resistance every centimetre. This density of data reveals thin sand lenses, soft clay layers, and the exact depth to competent rock, information that standard SPTs can miss. The test also generates empirical parameters directly usable in pile design: undrained shear strength in clays, relative density in sands, and constrained modulus for settlement calculations. When the target is a shallow footing on the Grimethorpe or Park Springs development areas, we often pair the CPT with footings analysis to validate bearing pressures without over-designing the foundation. For deeper mineworkings and old shafts, the CPT can sense voids or collapsed ground where traditional grouting treatments may be required before construction proceeds.

Local considerations

BS EN 1997-2 (Eurocode 7 Part 2) mandates that ground investigation must be sufficient to establish the ground model and characteristic values for design. In Barnsley, the risk of encountering unmapped mine entries, variable fill thickness, or gas from old workings makes a sparse investigation a genuine liability. The CPT provides a level of detail that satisfies the rigorous requirements of the Coal Authority's permitting process for development on the coalfield. A refusal on a buried sandstone boulder or a hard ironstone band within the Pennine Middle Coal Measures can be misinterpreted as bedrock; our operators cross-reference refusal depth with local geological mapping and offset soundings to confirm the true rockhead. Failing to identify a soft clay layer just a metre thick between the glacial till and the mudstone cap can lead to differential settlement and expensive remedial work. The CPT profile de-risks the foundation design by revealing exactly what lies beneath the rig, metre by metre.

Associated technical services

01

CPTu Profiling and Pore Pressure Dissipation Testing

We deploy a tracked 20-tonne CPT rig capable of penetrating the stiff glacial tills and weathered mudstone common across the Barnsley area. The piezocone (CPTu) measures cone resistance, sleeve friction, and pore water pressure simultaneously. Dissipation tests at target depths estimate the coefficient of consolidation and equilibrium groundwater pressure, critical for assessing consolidation settlement in the alluvial deposits of the Dearne Valley.

02

CPT-Based Foundation Parameter Report

We convert the raw CPT data into design-ready soil parameters: undrained shear strength profiles for clay, relative density and friction angle for sand, and constrained modulus for immediate settlement calculations. Each report includes a stratigraphic log with interpreted geology, soil behaviour type classification charts (Robertson, 1990), and direct design recommendations for shallow and piled foundations in accordance with BS EN 1997-1.

Frequently asked questions

How deep can a CPT rig push in Barnsley's ground conditions?

Our 20-tonne CPT rig typically reaches 15 to 25 metres in the glacial till and Coal Measures sequences of the Barnsley area. The limiting factor is usually refusal on a hard sandstone bed or ironstone band within the Pennine Middle Coal Measures. In the alluvial deposits along the River Dearne, we can often push deeper, up to the full 30-metre rod string, as the silts and sands offer lower resistance. We assess ground conditions before mobilisation using available borehole records and geological maps to give a realistic target depth for each site.

What's the cost of a CPT investigation in Barnsley?

CPT testing in the Barnsley and wider South Yorkshire area typically ranges from £120 to £210 per linear metre pushed, depending on the number of soundings, the depth required, and whether pore pressure dissipation tests are needed. A single-day mobilisation for two or three soundings to 15 metres is the most cost-efficient setup. We provide a fixed-price quotation after reviewing your site location, access constraints, and project scope.

Can a CPT detect old mine workings beneath a site?

The CPT can identify indirect signs of shallow mine workings or collapsed shafts: zones of very low cone resistance, sudden loss of rod thrust, or erratic pore pressure response. However, a CPT alone cannot fully characterise old workings. For sites in Barnsley's coalfield, we combine CPT profiling with rotary drilling, downhole camera surveys, and grouting records from the Coal Authority to build a complete picture. The CPT is excellent at mapping the intact ground around the workings and identifying zones of collapse or void migration.

How quickly can we get results from a CPT day on site?

The CPT data is recorded digitally in real time, and we can provide a preliminary stratigraphic log and key parameters (undrained shear strength, relative density) on the same day as the fieldwork. The full interpretative report, including soil behaviour type classification, dissipation analysis, and foundation design parameters, is typically delivered within three to five working days. For tight programme deadlines on Barnsley construction projects, we can fast-track the reporting to 48 hours.
