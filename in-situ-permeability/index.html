Field Permeability Testing (Lefranc/Lugeon) in Barnsley

Together, we solve the challenges of tomorrow.

LEARN MORE →

The setup begins with a rotary rig placing a cased borehole into the Coal Measures strata common beneath Barnsley. A single packer is lowered to the test zone for a Lugeon assessment in fractured rock, or a slotted standpipe is grouted in for Lefranc tests in overlying drift deposits. We measure water take against pressure steps using a calibrated flow meter and pressure transducer at the collar. The data logs directly onto a tablet on site. This equipment configuration is standard for our crews working across South Yorkshire ground conditions, from the sandstone outcrops west of the M1 to the alluvial silts along the River Dearne.

A single Lugeon value above 10 in the Middle Coal Measures flags the need for contact grouting before any shaft excavation.

Our approach and scope

Ground conditions shift noticeably between the shallow sandstone bedrock in the Dodworth area and the deeper clay and silt layers found toward Worsbrough. In Dodworth, Lugeon testing in the Penistone Flags often yields low lugeon values below 3, indicating tight joints suitable for standard foundation drainage. Over in Worsbrough, Lefranc tests in the glacial till show variable permeability, frequently requiring extended test phases to reach steady-state flow. Understanding this contrast is critical. We regularly pair this data with a grain-size analysis from the same borehole to correlate hydraulic conductivity with fines content, and use test pit logging to confirm the depth of weathered rock before setting packer positions.

Local ground factors

Barnsley's historical mining legacy creates specific risks. We often encounter collapsed mine workings or backfilled shafts that create artificial drainage paths, skewing permeability results by 2 to 3 orders of magnitude. A test that shows excessively high water take might not indicate permeable soil; it could be a void intercept. Our crew checks against historical mining records from the Coal Authority before interpreting results. Another practical point: testing in weathered mudstone requires careful packer placement. Seal the packer in the weathered zone and you get unrealistic leakage around the seal. We always set the packer at least 0.5 m into competent rock, confirmed by the driller's log.

Other technical services

Lugeon packer testing

Five-stage pressure test in rock boreholes following Houlsby's method. Flow vs. pressure curves generated on site to identify laminar, turbulent, or washout behaviour in the Coal Measures.

02

Lefranc variable head test

Falling head test in soil or very weak rock. Water level recovery monitored with a pressure transducer until equilibrium or rate is established, per BS 5930.

03

Soakaway infiltration testing

Trial pit percolation tests for Sustainable Drainage Systems design. Constant head method in trial pits up to 3 m deep, referenced to BRE Digest 365.

04

Permeability profiling in boreholes

Multiple isolated test intervals in a single borehole to build a depth profile of hydraulic conductivity, essential for dewatering design in open-pit mining and deep foundations.

Quick answers

What is the difference between a Lefranc test and a Lugeon test?

Lefranc tests measure permeability in soil or very weak rock using a simple slotted casing without a packer. Lugeon tests use an inflatable packer to isolate a specific section of rock borehole and apply pressure in steps. The Lugeon method quantifies joint conductivity and identifies hydraulic behaviour such as dilation or washout, which the Lefranc test cannot detect.

How much does a field permeability test cost in Barnsley?

The cost for a Lefranc or Lugeon test in Barnsley typically ranges from £480 to £910, depending on borehole depth, number of test intervals, and whether a drilling rig must be mobilised separately. Each additional test zone within the same borehole adds a reduced increment.

Which test do I need for a soakaway design in Barnsley?

For soakaway design under Building Regulations Part H, you generally need a trial pit infiltration test referenced to BRE Digest 365. However, if the site is underlain by Coal Measures sandstone, a Lugeon test in a borehole may be required to demonstrate adequate drainage at depth, especially where the water table is low.

How long does a single permeability test take on site?

A single packer test in rock takes about 45-60 minutes for the full five-pressure stage procedure. A Lefranc falling head test in soil can take from 30 minutes to over two hours, depending on the permeability of the material. Low conductivity clays require extended monitoring to reach a reliable steady-state reading.
